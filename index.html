USA Guide

Main Visa: F-1 (academic) or M-1 (vocational)

Requirements
Acceptance letter from SEVP-approved school
I-20 form issued by institution
Proof of funds for first year (tuition + living)
SEVIS fee + visa interview approval

Work Rules
Up to 20 hrs/week on-campus during semesters
CPT (Curricular Practical Training) – internships related to major
OPT (Optional Practical Training) – up to 12 months post-study, or 24-month STEM extension

Path After Study
Transition to H-1B work visa or further studies
Green card possible through employment or marriage later

Pros – world-class universities, research funding, high job potential
Cons – expensive, visa interviews strict, H-1B lottery uncertain


UK Guide

Main Visa: Student Route (formerly Tier 4)

Requirements
CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies) from licensed university
Proof of funds (~£1,334/month in London)
English test (IELTS/TOEFL)
Tuberculosis test if required

Work Rules
20 hrs/week during term, full-time during breaks
No self-employment or freelance work

After Study
Graduate Visa: 2 years post-study work permit (3 years for PhD)
Can switch to Skilled Worker Visa after finding an eligible job

Pros – clear post-study path, English-speaking, global exposure
Cons – high rent, PR requires long stay


Canada Guide

Main Visa: Study Permit

Requirements
Acceptance from Designated Learning Institution (DLI)
Proof of funds (tuition + $20,635 CAD/year for living)
Medical exam + biometrics

Work Rules
20 hrs/week during term, full-time in breaks
Off-campus work allowed
Eligible for Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) up to 3 years

After Study
PGWP experience helps qualify for Express Entry or Provincial Nominee PR
One of the easiest transitions from student to PR

Pros – clear PR route, safe environment, affordable tuition
Cons – harsh winters, regional job demand differences


Australia Guide

Main Visa: Subclass 500

Requirements
Offer from CRICOS-registered institution
Proof of funds (~AUD $24,505/year for living)
OSHC (health insurance)
English test (IELTS/TOEFL/PTE)

Work Rules
48 hrs/fortnight during term
Unlimited hours during holidays

After Study
Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485): 2–4 years post-study work right depending on degree
PR via Skilled Migration points system

Pros – strong job rights, PR friendly, multicultural society
Cons – high living costs, strict attendance and progress rules


Europe Guide

Main Visa: National “D-Type” Study Visa

Requirements
University admission letter
Proof of funds (approx €11,000/year in blocked account for Germany)
Health insurance, accommodation, travel insurance

Work Rules
Germany: 120 full or 240 half days per year
France: 964 hrs/year
Netherlands: 16 hrs/week or full-time in summer

After Study
Germany/France/Netherlands offer job-seeker visas (12–18 months) to find work
Leads to EU Blue Card and eventual PR

Pros – affordable tuition, post-study opportunities, cultural exposure
Cons – language barriers, varying immigration rules by country


FAQs

How early should I apply for a student visa?

Start 4–6 months before your program start date; embassy/consulate wait times vary.

Can I work while studying?

Most destinations allow limited part-time work with conditions (hours caps, term-time rules); check your visa rules.

Our Practice Areas

Student Visa Applications

Comprehensive assistance with student visa applications for all major study destinations including USA (F-1), Canada (Study Permit), UK (Tier 4/Student), Australia (Student 500), and more. We handle document preparation, application submission, and interview coaching.

University Application Support

Legal review of university applications, scholarship applications, and admission contracts. We ensure all documentation meets legal requirements and protect your interests throughout the admission process.

Work Authorization & OPT

Guidance on work authorization options including Optional Practical Training (OPT), Curricular Practical Training (CPT), and post-graduation work permits. We help you understand your rights and maximize your career opportunities.

Housing & Accommodation Law

Legal assistance with rental agreements, housing contracts, and tenant rights in your study destination. We review lease agreements and help resolve housing disputes to ensure safe and legal accommodation.

Family & Dependent Visas

Support for bringing family members including spouses and children on dependent visas. We handle the complex requirements and documentation needed for family reunification during your studies.

Post-Graduation Immigration

Strategic planning for permanent residency and long-term immigration options after graduation. We help you transition from student status to professional work authorization and eventual permanent residence.

Pre-Departure Legal Compliance Checklist

Document verification, insurance requirements, housing legality, financial compliance, and travel documentation checks prior to departure.

Work Visa & Employment Contract Review
