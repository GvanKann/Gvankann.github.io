Immigration and Foreigners Act, 2025: A New Era of Border Security

The new Immigration and Foreigners Act, 2025, which came into force on September 1, 2025, replaces four colonial-era immigration laws and introduces stringent penalties for forged travel documents and other violations, aiming to reinforce border security and immigration control.

April 1, 2026

Income-tax Act, 2025: Modernizing Direct Taxation

The new Income-tax Act, 2025, passed by both houses of Parliament in mid-August 2025 and receiving Presidential assent on August 21, 2025, modernizes direct taxation by replacing the 1961 Act, simplifies compliance, and introduces a unified "Tax Year" concept effective from April 1, 2026.

August 2025

National Sports Governance Act, 2025: Transforming Sports Administration

The National Sports Governance Act, 2025, passed by both houses of Parliament in August 2025, establishes a statutory structure for the governance of sports bodies, embeds ethical codes, dispute resolution mechanisms, and aligns with Olympic and Paralympic frameworks.

Read More →

August 21, 2025

Mines and Minerals Amendment Act, 2025: Strengthening Critical Mineral Strategy

The Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Amendment Act, 2025, receiving Presidential assent on August 21, 2025, introduces mineral exchanges, allows limited lease extensions (up to 10–30%), expands the mandate of the National Mineral Exploration and Development Trust, and strengthens critical mineral strategy.

Read More →

August 21, 2025

Online Gaming Bill, 2025: Regulating the Digital Gaming Landscape

The Promotion and Regulation of Online Gaming Bill, 2025, passed by Lok Sabha on August 20 and Rajya Sabha on August 21, 2025, categorizes games into e-sports, educational/social games, and money-based games, with e-sports encouraged and betting-based games banned, imposing fines up to ₹1 crore and jail up to three years.

August 11, 2025

Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 2025: Modernizing Maritime Transport

The Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 2025, passed by Lok Sabha on March 28, 2025, and Rajya Sabha on August 11, 2025, replaces the 1925 law, updates the legal framework for maritime cargo transport to align with Hague–Visby and Hamburg Rules, and addresses multimodal transport concerns.
